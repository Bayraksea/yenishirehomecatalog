TR

YenishireHome offers a peaceful and comfortable stay intertwined with nature. It is not only a place to stay but also a perfect starting point for exploring the rich cultural and natural heritage of the region.

This guide aims to enrich your stay by providing information on the historical landmarks, breathtaking natural landscapes, lakes, canyons, caves, and hiking trails located in the vicinity of YenishireHome.

Within a 1 to 1.5-hour driving distance, you can find a wide array of routes—from Seljuk caravan roads to the mesmerizing foothills of the Taurus Mountains, from crystal-clear lakes to hidden waterfalls.

While staying at YenishireHome, you won’t just rest; you’ll also enjoy the privilege of discovering the history, culture, and nature of this unique region.

To enhance your journey and exploration, we have included suggested routes to some of the area’s most fascinating natural and historical attractions:

1st Route: Melikler Plateau – Aksu – Sagalassos

From YenishireHome, heading toward Isparta, you will reach Melikler Plateau in about 20 minutes. Pınargözü Cave, one of Turkey’s longest caves, is located here. In the same area, hiking and summit ascents can be made around Karagöl, Yeşil Lake, and Mount Dedegöl.

About 20 minutes from the plateau lies Yaka Canyon, a perfect route for hiking and canyoning.

Further along the Isparta route, you can visit Zindan Cave and Başpınar Nature Park, which are located near the Aksu district center.

If you continue through Eğirdir toward Kovada Lake, you will find unique natural areas such as Kasnak Oak Nature Reserve, Kovada Lake National Park, and Yazılı Canyon Nature Park. Along the same route, Sagalassos Ancient City stands as one of the most impressive archaeological sites in the region.

2nd Route: Kubadabad Palace – Kızıldağ – Pisidia Ancient City

Following the shoreline of Lake Beyşehir toward Şarkikaraağaç, your first stop is Kubadabad Palace, an outstanding example of Anatolian Seljuk architecture offering a breathtaking lake view.

Next, visit Kızıldağ National Park, located very close to the Şarkikaraağaç district center, and then continue to Pisidia Antiocheia Ancient City in Yalvaç.

3rd Route: Valley of Storks – Beyşehir – Eflatunpınar – Fasıllar

While heading to Beyşehir, stop by Yeşildağ Village’s Valley of Storks, where you can observe migrating storks in their natural habitat.

Karaburun Beach by Lake Beyşehir is ideal for a refreshing swim in Turkey’s largest freshwater lake.

Once in the district center, you can visit Seljuk monuments, explore the historical bazaar, and sample local delicacies. Around 10 km from the center, enjoy a boat tour in the Water Lily Garden, then head another 10 km to see the Eflatunpınar Hittite Monument. Finally, visit the Fasıllar Hittite Monument, located about 20 km from the center.

4th Route: Çamlık Caves – Ormana – Köprülü Canyon

Heading toward Antalya, your first stop is Derebucak Çamlık National Park. Then, visit Ormana Village, famous for its traditional button houses.

In the highlands along the way, you can observe wild horses in their natural environment. From Serik, a road leads directly to Beşkonak, where Köprülü Canyon National Park is located.

Here, you can enjoy water sports in one of Turkey’s best rafting spots and explore historical and natural landmarks such as the ancient city of Selge and Tazı Canyon.

1st Route: Melikler Plateau – Aksu – Sagalassos

🗺️ Route 1: Mount Dedegöl and Surroundings

Mount Dedegöl is located within the Yenişarbademli district of Isparta Province. It is composed mainly of limestone and rises to 2998 meters. The mountain lies within Kızıldağ National Park.

The area offers ideal conditions for hiking, camping, and climbing. Thousands of mountaineers participate annually in the Mountaineering Festival held in Melikler Plateau. Although technically non-challenging, reaching the summit requires a long day hike. From the camp area, the summit hike is approximately 10 km and takes about 4 hours to ascend and 4 hours to descend, depending on group and individual pace.

The best season to climb is from June to the end of September, though mountaineering and ski touring are possible in winter.

In addition to Melikler Plateau, Pınargözü Cave, Karagöl, Yeşilgöl, Yaka Canyon, Eldere Region, and Kuzukulağı Plateau are frequently visited by campers, climbers, and hikers.

🌄 Melikler Plateau

Located at the foot of Mount Dedegöl, the plateau is about 15 km from Yenişarbademli town. Surrounded by pine trees, this flat area sits at 1735 meters above sea level and offers a perfect setting for camping, hiking, and mountain climbing.

The annual Mountaineering Festival held in May at Melikler Plateau and Mount Dedegöl is a nationally significant event. Furthermore, Melikler Plateau is one of the darkest measured areas in Turkey, making it ideal for stargazing. The Sky Observation Festival held here every July or August draws amateur and professional astronomers alike.

Besides camping and stargazing, alternative activities include climbing to Mount Dedegöl, Karagöl, and Yeşilgöl, and hiking to Pınargözü Cave and Yaka Canyon from the plateau.

📍 Location

🌄 Pınargözü Cave

Located 11 kilometers west of the Yenişarbademli district, Pınargözü Cave is one of the most significant natural formations in Turkey. A powerful spring with a flow rate of 778 liters per second emerges from the cave’s entrance. Inside, the cave features numerous siphons, waterfalls, and impressive underground cascades.

Thanks to years of exploration, approximately 16 kilometers of the cave have been mapped, although the full extent remains undiscovered. The furthest known point lies 660 meters above the cave’s entrance level—making it the deepest known rise inside a cave in Turkey. This alone makes Pınargözü the country’s most expansive cave in terms of both length and elevation difference.

The cave houses pools, waterfalls, dripstone basins, and all varieties of stalactite formations. At the entrance, a powerful wind blows at speeds reaching 150–160 km/h. With its length, internal elevation gain, water temperature (around 3–4°C), and wind speed, Pınargözü is considered Turkey’s largest cave. It is also regarded as one of the longest caves in Europe, attracting speleologists and adventurers from around the world.

📍 Location

🌄 Yaka Canyon

Located approximately 7.5 kilometers from Melikler Plateau, Yaka Canyon has a total length of about 4 kilometers and a width varying between 1.5 and 2 meters. The vertical rock walls rise steeply, starting from 30 meters and exceeding 100 meters in height. Within the canyon, there are numerous waterfalls and natural pools. With its exceptional natural formation, the canyon offers an accessible hiking route that requires minimal equipment. However, during rainy periods, the narrowness of the canyon corridor can cause water levels to rise rapidly, posing a risk to visitors.

🌄 Karagöl Glacial Lake

Located east of Mount Dedegöl, Karagöl is a glacial lake with an area of 2,500 square meters. Karagöl boasts beautiful natural beauty, and is notable for the Dedegöl flower, found only around Karagöl and at the foot of Mount Dedegöl. Camping is possible around the lake, and you may occasionally encounter wild horses.

📍 Location

🌄 Yeşilgöl ( Green Lake )

Located on the western side of Mount Dedegöl, Yeşilgöl is a seasonal lake that forms in spring as the snow melts and gradually recedes during the summer months. It remains a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. The hiking trail from Melikler Plateau to the lake takes approximately 3 hours. Around the lake, there are suitable spots for camping as well as a stream fed by snowmelt.

📍 Location

🌄 Kuzukulağı Plateau

Located near Mount Dedegöl, Kuzukulağı Plateau is one of Turkey’s most important destinations for multi-pitch climbing. Throughout the year, thousands of outdoor sports enthusiasts visit the area for activities such as rock climbing, alpine climbing, sport climbing, mountaineering, ski touring, camping, hiking, mountain biking, and motocross.

The region hosts the Kuzukulağı Outdoor Sports Festival every summer, drawing large numbers of local and international visitors. With climbing routes ranging in length from 20 to 600 meters—designed by both Turkish and international route setters—the area offers world-class climbing experiences.

Home to a wide variety of endemic plant species and wildlife, the plateau also features many scenic hiking trails and untouched natural landscapes, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

📍 Location

🌄 Zindan Cave

Located about 2 kilometers northeast of the Aksu district, Zindan Cave lies on the southern slope of the Aksu Stream gorge, at an elevation of 1300 meters above sea level. With a total length of 765 meters, the cave is fully illuminated, making walking and exploring inside easy and comfortable.

The cave, which is semi-active, features various dripstone formations, flowstones, rimstones, and an underground stream that has carved out unique stalactites and erosional features. In some sections, miniature canyons have formed within the cave. At the end of the cave is a section known as "the bath," where the floor is covered with uniquely shaped calcite crystals that resemble mosaics.

Chemical analyses show that the water flowing inside the cave has high levels of calcium and magnesium.

At the cave entrance, mosaics created from black, white, and red stone tesserae depict the head of Eurymedon (the river god of Köprü Stream), flanked by dolphin motifs and winged male figures at the top and bottom.

📍 Location

🌄 Başpınar Nature Park

Located within the borders of Aksu District on the road to Sorgun Dam, Başpınar Nature Park is 8 kilometers from Aksu town center. It can be reached by following the road from Aksu to Zindan Cave, passing the cave and several fish farms along the way. The highland area contains a natural spring that can be used as a source of drinking water.

📍 Location

🌄 Kovada Lake National Park

Located 29 kilometers south of Eğirdir, Kovada Lake is a relatively shallow body of water. Its unique richness in flora and diversity of wildlife, combined with its great potential for outdoor recreation and leisure, have earned the area its designation as a national park. Covering an area of 6,534 hectares, the park offers opportunities for nature exploration, camping, hiking, scenic viewing, birdwatching, and climbing.

📍 Location

🌄 Kasnak Oak Nature Reserve

Located within the boundaries of Yukarıgökdere Village in the Eğirdir district of Isparta Province, the Kasnak Oak Nature Reserve was designated as a protected area on July 27, 1987. It is the only known natural forest formed by the endemic Quercus vulcanica (Kasnak Oak) species, covering an area of 1,300 hectares.

Renowned for its rich biodiversity, the reserve is home to 442 identified plant species, 69 of which are endemic to the region. What makes this area unique is that it hosts the only forest ecosystem in the world dominated by the Kasnak Oak tree.

📍 Location

🌄 Yazılı Canyon Nature Park

Located 20 kilometers from the district of Sütçüler, Yazılı Canyon Nature Park covers an area of 600 hectares. The canyon’s side walls feature Byzantine-era worship areas, altars, and inscriptions, which is why the canyon is named “Yazılı” (Inscribed) Canyon. The depth of the canyon ranges between 100 and 400 meters.

Situated within the borders of Çandır Village, the park was officially registered as a nature park in 1989. Known for its rich vegetation and wildlife, the park’s breathtaking natural beauty makes it a popular destination for tourists.

The canyon holds significant historical importance as it lies along the ancient “King’s Road” and contains temples and rock inscriptions. Saint Paul is believed to have passed through this canyon while traveling from Perge to Pisidia Antiocheia.

Within the park, there are camping areas suitable for visitors to rest and relax.

📍 Location

🌄 Sagalassos Ancient City

Sagalassos Ancient City is located in the Ağlasun district of Burdur Province. Built on the slopes of Akdağ, the city sits at an elevation between 1500 and 1700 meters above sea level. During the Roman period, it was one of the most important cities in the Pisidia region.

Most of the structures in Sagalassos date back to the Roman era. The earliest settlement traces go back approximately 12,000 years. The city was abandoned in the early 13th century AD after the Seljuks eliminated the last Byzantine military fortress settlement.

Sagalassos is one of the best-preserved ancient cities in Anatolia. At the entrance to the city, there is the courtyard and halls of a large city mansion resembling a palace. The lower part of the city is dominated by the grand Roman Imperial Bath.

Around the Lower Agora, you can find the remains of the agora fountain, the Temple of Apollo Klarios, and the Hadrian Fountain. Many monuments have been excavated and restored around the Upper Agora.

The city’s most prominent structure is the Antonine Fountain, whose waters still flow today. Among the ruins are two monumental arches leading to the city square, honorary columns at each corner of the agora, the city hall, the city council building, a church, and the heroon decorated with reliefs of dancing girls.

The remains of the ancient library and theater have also survived to the present day.

2nd Route: Kubadabad Palace – Kızıldağ – Pisidia Ancient City

🌄 Kubadabad Palace

The Kubadabad Seljuk Palace Complex is the only surviving Seljuk palace structure to this day, built by the order of Sultan Alaaddin Keykubad I (1220-1236). This palace is considered a precursor to the Ottoman-era palaces such as Edirne and Topkapı Palaces.

The complex is located right next to Lake Beyşehir, at the foothills of the Anamas Mountains. Excavations have been ongoing since 1980. The palace ruins are adorned with dazzling tiles that decorate museums today. The motifs on these tiles depict hunting scenes, mystical beliefs, the sultan, his dignitaries, and servants.

Rare tile artifacts excavated from the palace are exhibited in various museums in Konya, especially the Karatay Museum.

📍 Location

🌄 Kızıldağ National Park

Declared a national park in 1993, Kızıldağ National Park is located within the boundaries of Şarkikaraağaç district in Isparta Province. The park is renowned for its blue cedar forests, which produce abundant oxygen. Thanks to this, the park’s clean air is rich in oxygen and is considered a healing environment for people with lung, asthma, and respiratory problems.

Within the park, there are walking trails of 3, 6, and 9 kilometers in length. Visitors can also find rentable bungalows, container houses, numerous camping sites for tents, and picnic areas.

📍 Location

🌄 Psidia Antiokheia Ancient City

Pisidia Antiokheia Ancient City is located about 1 kilometer northeast of Yalvaç district in Isparta Province, situated in the Lakes Region where the Mediterranean, Aegean, and Central Anatolia regions intersect. The city is built on sloping and partly rocky terrain, bordered to the north and west by the Hisarardı road and to the southeast by the Antiochos (Hisarardı) River.

The city, which can be described with a trapezoidal plan, contains ruins from the Roman and Byzantine periods. Excavations (1914–1924) and research (1982–1983) have fully determined the locations of the city walls. The main entrance to the city is on the west side.

To the north of the city, there are remains of structures such as Aqueducts, a Nymphaeum, a Bathhouse, and a Palestra. The center of the city includes a Theater, the Tiberius Square, a Propylon, and the Augustus Temple area.

Pisidia Antiokheia is considered a pilgrimage site as it was visited by Saint Paul, who spread Christianity in the region. Therefore, the St. Paul Church located here is also regarded as one of the pilgrimage centers.

3rd Route: Valley of Storks – Beyşehir – Eflatunpınar – Fasıllar

🌄 Beyşehir Lake National Park

Beyşehir Lake, Turkey’s largest freshwater source, hosts two separate national parks: Beyşehir and Kızıldağ. Covering an area of 88,750 hectares, Beyşehir Lake National Park is the largest national park in Turkey.

The forest formations in Beyşehir Lake National Park consist of species such as juniper, black pine, fir, cedar, and oak. These trees often extend to the lake’s shores, creating a visually stunning landscape around Beyşehir Lake’s bays and inlets.

The park’s exceptional landscape beauty, its role as a sanctuary for migratory birds, the presence of lake shores suitable for water sports, and the cultural richness dating back to the Seljuk period all contribute to the significant value of Beyşehir Lake National Park.

The islands within the lake are important breeding and nesting grounds for waterfowl species. They also serve as wintering and breeding habitats for diving birds, swans, cormorants, certain heron species, and ducks.

Additionally, the alkaline quality of the lake water supports fish species such as carp and perch. The presence of turtles and snakes enriches the lake’s fauna.

The peak visitation season is between May and October. During this period, the park offers day-use camping and caravan opportunities along its lush green shoreline areas. For other accommodation options, visitors can use facilities in the nearby district center.

🌄 Beyşehir

With a history spanning approximately 8,000 years, Beyşehir has been home to many civilizations. It holds a distinguished place due to its historical structures inherited from this deep past, its magnificent natural beauties, and its rich cultural heritage.

Located about 10 km northwest of Beyşehir, the Erbaba Mound is a Neolithic settlement dating back roughly 8,000 years, providing some of the earliest archaeological evidence for the region. As indicated by the Beşik of the Wolf (Kurt Beşiği) and the Eflatunpınar Monument, the city’s most significant period is believed to have been during the Hittite Era. Between 2000 BCE, the Hittites left enduring monuments at Eflatunpınar and Fasıllar.

Between 78 and 74 BCE, the city came under Roman rule, and efforts to make the city a Turkish homeland began during the Anatolian Seljuk period. After the Mongol invasions, the city was known as Viranşehir. With the establishment of the Eşrefoğlu Beylik, the city went through various phases before finally being named Beyşehir.

Key places to visit in Beyşehir include Eşrefoğlu Mosque and its complex, the Stone Bridge, Kubadabad Palace, Eflatunpınar Hittite Monument, Fasıllar Monument, the Water Lily Garden, and Karaburun Beach.

📍 Location

🌄 Eşrefoğlu Mosque

Located in the city center of Beyşehir, Eşrefoğlu Mosque is one of the oldest structures from the Seljuk Beylik period. It was originally commissioned by Seljuk Sultan Sancar in 1134 and later rebuilt in its current form in 1297 by Eşrefoğlu Süleyman Bey. The mosque is still open for worship and visitation.

This 7-century-old mosque holds a special place in Turkish architectural history as the only wooden mosque where various decorative arts such as stone, brick, tile, and colorful painting are intensely combined. It is known as the largest flat-ceilinged mosque supported by wooden columns. One of its unique features is the "karlık," a 4-5 meter deep well located at the center of the mosque. It is believed that the karlık was built to prevent the mosque from decaying. The snow that accumulates in the well melts slowly, and the resulting humidity helps preserve the wooden structure’s longevity.

In 2012, UNESCO included Eşrefoğlu Mosque on the World Heritage Tentative List, and in 2023 it was officially inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. The mosque complex, built between 1296 and 1299 during the Eşrefoğlu Beylik era by Eşrefoğlu Seyfettin Süleyman Bey, also includes a tomb, caravanserai, and bathhouse.

Its grand entrance, Seljuk-style tile mosaic mihrab, exquisitely carved wooden minbar made with the kundekari technique, wooden columns, ornately decorated wooden ceiling with fine calligraphy, and countless other beauties make it resemble a museum. Located next to Beyşehir Lake, this mosque is a must-visit site.

Around the mosque, there are many historic structures including the Eşrefoğlu Süleyman Bey Bazaar, bathhouse, tomb, historic city gate, and İsmail Ağa Madrasa.

📍 Location

🌄 Fasıllar Hittite Monument

The monument is one of the largest rock monuments in the world and dates back to the Hittite period. The Fasıllar Monument depicts the Great God between two lions at a mountain temple. It is located on the western slope of a hill south of Fasıllar Village, which belongs to the Beyşehir district of Konya.

The monument measures 2.25 x 2.75 x 8.30 meters and is carved on a 20-square-meter surface of a massive 72-ton rock. It consists of two stacked gods and, beside the lower god, a pair of lion reliefs.

The Battle of Kadesh, fought in 1243 BC between the Hittites and Egyptians, was a significant turning point in history, ending with no clear victor. The subsequent Kadesh Peace Treaty between Hittite King Muwatalli and Egyptian Pharaoh Ramesses II is considered the first written treaty in history.

The Fasıllar Monument was created to depict this historic event from the Hittite perspective.

📍 Location

🌄 Eflatunpınar Hittite Water Monument

The monument dates back to the last quarter of the 13th century BC. It stands out from other Hittite rock monuments due to its original stone craftsmanship, the composition of its reliefs, and its design as an open-air temple.

The Eflatunpınar Monument is not carved directly on natural rock; rather, it was meticulously constructed by fitting together andesite blocks cut to fit perfectly. It consists of a large pool built over a natural water source, with horizontal water channels running parallel to the pool’s walls to allow water to flow into the pool.

On the rectangular-shaped stones, reliefs depict figures of gods and goddesses.

📍 Location

🌄 Valley of Storks

An important stop for nature lovers in Beyşehir district of Konya is the Storks Hill located in the Adaköy neighborhood of Beyşehir. The storks here have recently attracted great interest from both local and international visitors and nature enthusiasts. As a result, a viewing terrace was recently constructed on the hill to observe the storks, and this area has gained fame under the name “Storks Hill.”

“Storks Hill” hosts about 200 stork nests, stretching approximately 600 meters long and 80 meters wide, covering an area of about 40 decares (4 hectares). The hill is a historic cemetery completely covered with juniper trees, and almost every juniper tree top has a stork’s nest. Each nest houses 4 to 5 storks, including their chicks.

Becoming a symbol of Adaköy, Storks Hill welcomes approximately 500 to 600 storks annually, with some variation from year to year.

📍 Location

🌄 Water Lily Garden

Located along the shores of Çiftlik Neighborhood by Lake Beyşehir, the Water Lily Garden captivates everyone with its unique scenery and peaceful nature. Stretching approximately 7 kilometers, it is the largest water lily garden in Turkey. The area’s rich biodiversity has made it a favorite spot for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts alike.

📍 Location

🌄 Karaburun Beach

Karaburun Beach is located within Beyşehir Lake National Park and stands out with its three-kilometer-long shoreline. The beach is known for its fine sandy texture and offers opportunities for water sports and camping. Additionally, there are separate sections designated for singles, families, and women.

4th Route: Çamlık Caves – Ormana – Köprülü Canyon

🌄 Derebucak Çamlık Caves National Park

Declared a national park in 2022, Derebucak Çamlık Caves National Park covers an area of 1,147 hectares and includes the Körükini, Suluin, Balatini, Asmacıini, Çocuk Attıkları Delik, Saklı Uçurum, Derevend, Baraj, Eski Düden, Dede Tarlası Düdeni, Gavur Beşiği, and Dölek Düdeni caves. Balatini Cave is one of Turkey’s longest caves that can be fully explored. Suluin Cave, especially popular in the summer months when tourism peaks, is a beautifully decorated cave suitable for boat tours. The Derevend and Döllüköğü caves also attract visitors with their significant geomorphological features. Mastaltı, Dede Tarla, and Çocuk Attıkları Delik caves are ideal for visitors seeking a guided vertical or semi-inclined caving experience.

📍 Location

🌄 Düğmeli Houses

These unique traditional houses, located in Akseki, İbradı, Ormana, and Ürünlü districts of Antalya, reflect Ottoman architecture. They are constructed using cedar tar wood carefully cut and interlocked as a skeleton framework. The buildings are built by stacking stones without using any mortar or cement. Because the parts of the wooden skeleton that remain exposed on the outside look like buttons, these houses are called “Düğmeli” (buttoned) houses, also known as “pişduvan” houses.

📍 Location

🌄 Köprülü Canyon National Park

Köprülü Canyon National Park is located 63 kilometers northwest of Manavgat district in Antalya Province. Situated within the boundaries of Manavgat, the area includes the Köprüçay (Köprü) River and the historical Selge Ancient City, covering 366,140 decares. The region was declared a National Park in 1973.

The Köprüçay River forms a deep valley between Bolasan Village and Beşkonak, with a length of 14 kilometers and heights exceeding 100 meters, making it one of Turkey’s longest canyons. The canyon walls are steep and nearly impossible to climb. Fed by underground waters, Köprüçay is one of the most beautiful natural recreation areas in Turkey.

The ancient city of Selge, located on the mountainous terrain west of the river, along with nearby castles, aqueducts, Roman-era bridges, and historic roads, adds to the importance of Köprülü Canyon.

Additionally, within the park’s 400-hectare area lies the largest natural cedar forest in the entire Mediterranean region. Visitors can also admire interesting geomorphological formations such as the Adam (Devil) Rocks and the Tazı Canyon.

Thanks to its rich natural, historical, and cultural assets, Köprülü Canyon National Park offers numerous activity options for visitors. These include rafting, canoeing, and canyoning (canyon crossing) on Köprüçay River, one of Turkey’s most important rafting routes. Other activities include swimming, hiking, rock climbing, orienteering, cycling, fishing, botanical and wildlife observation, geological exploration, camping, photography, picnicking, jeep safaris, and plateau tours.

Visitors to the region can also include the Selge Ancient City within the park and part of the Saint Paul Trail, which partially runs through the National Park, in their tour programs.

Activities

🌄 Saint Paul Trail

Named after Saint Paul and his companions’ journey in AD 46 to spread Christianity, the Saint Paul Trail offers walkers an unforgettable experience by combining the natural beauty at the foothills of the Taurus Mountains with the region’s local culture and ancient cities.

The trail is approximately 500 km long. It begins as two branches near Perge and Aspendos close to Antalya, then joins at the Ancient City of Adada. From there, it passes through the foothills of Mount Davraz and Eğirdir, ending in Yalvaç district of Isparta.

The route includes paths reaching altitudes up to 2000 meters, where walkers can observe endemic species of the Taurus Mountains, local village life, various canyon formations, and ancient ruins. Compared to the Lycian Way, this route is wilder, starting at sea level and climbing up to 2200 meters. There are also two optional peaks along the route reaching about 2800 meters.

The ideal time for hiking is from April to May and September to October.

🌄 Keykubat Migration and Caravan Route

The Keykubat Caravan Route Project is a cultural, historical, and touristic route based on the caravan roads and caravanserais built during the Anatolian Seljuk State period. The project is named after Seljuk Sultan Alaeddin Keykubad I and follows the palaces, inns, and roads constructed during his reign.

Its purpose is to bring the grandeur and commercial-touristic heritage of the Seljuk era to today’s travelers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers, while also promoting local development and cultural tourism.

Stretching from Konya to Alanya, the Keykubat Caravan Route has been a crossroads of civilizations for centuries, carrying traces of cultures, trade, and beliefs. This path not only preserves the footprints of the past but also serves as a corridor of culture and nature featuring the unique landscapes of Anatolia.

Seljuk inns, stone bridges, and ancient ruins scattered along the foothills of the Taurus Mountains provide a historical backdrop at every step of the journey, while the region’s peaceful and enchanting nature offers visitors a calming atmosphere.

The Keykubat Caravan Route is an exceptional route for hikers and cycling enthusiasts. Along the way, the steep cliffs of the Blue Gorge Canyon, the cool waters of Aygırdibi Waterfall, and the clear flow of the Alara River transform this route into not just a historical journey but also a natural exploration experience.
